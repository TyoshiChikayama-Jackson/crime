The Social Issues in Violent Crime

A Look Into Crime Within the United States

Introduction

Violent crime in the United States has shifted over the years. Various factors contribute to these changes — social injustices, economic challenges, and household structures all play a role. These factors often correlate with specific events, such as the impact of COVID-19 on communities, racial divides, and levels of economic support. According to the FBI's data collected from over 10,000 reporters, there has been an overall decrease in violent crime rates.

  • Violent crime rates in the United States have fluctuated significantly over time.
  • Global, national, and local disruptions to society are among the largest drivers of crime.
  • Violent crime is not limited to the incidents officially reported to authorities.

Factors of Crime (General and Violent)

The following factors and identifiers for crime — especially violent crime — are recognized by the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI):

  • Population and Population Growth
  • Economic Conditions and Advancement
  • Community Funding and Modes of Transportation
  • Educational and Religious Characteristics
  • Law Enforcement Efficiency and Strength
  • Relations Between Society and Law Enforcement

Road to Solutions and Limitations

An increase in violent crime creates unsafe communities and raises demand for law enforcement funding. Over time, this lowers the quality of life in affected areas. Raising the cost of living has been proposed as one solution, but it has proven less effective than alternatives. A larger and more disputed solution involves increasing law enforcement funding. While crime rates have decreased alongside improved community stability, the debate over how to address violent crime continues.

  • Law enforcement funding, community investment, job training, and employment opportunities are key solutions.
  • Rising crime decreases the quality of communities, businesses, and local economies.
  • Solutions create conflict between advocates and critics across the political spectrum.
  • Any solution is likely to face opposition from some majority or authority group.

Ways to Get Involved

Crime occurs at every level in every community — there is no "perfect" neighborhood in the United States. However, actions can be taken to minimize the severity and frequency of crimes. People of any age or background can help build a safer neighborhood, from serving in nonprofit organizations to contacting elected representatives.

  • Volunteer with organizations whose mission is to support and strengthen the community.
  • Provide financial support through government programs or nonprofit organizations.
  • Invest time in educating youth about the causes and impacts of violent crime.
